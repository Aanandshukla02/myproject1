Chanakya, also known as Kautilya or Vishnugupta , was an ancient Indian philosopher, economist, strategist, and political advisor who lived in the 4th century BCE. He is best known as the author of the Arthashastra, an influential treatise on statecraft, economics, and political strategy. Chanakya was born in the ancient city of Taxila (in present-day Pakistan) and was a disciple of the great philosopher and teacher, Chanakya. He played a crucial role in the rise of the Mauryan Empire, serving as the chief advisor to Chandragupta Maurya, the founder of the empire. Chanakya's strategies and tactics were instrumental in defeating the powerful Nanda Empire and consolidating the Mauryan Empire. Chanakya was a great proponent of realpolitik, the idea that politics should be based on practical considerations rather than moral or ideological principles. He believed that a ruler should do whatever it takes to protect and expand his power, even if it meant using deceit, manipulation, or violence. Apart from the Arthashastra, Chanakya is also credited with composing the Chanakya Niti, a collection of aphorisms on life and governance. His teachings continue to be studied and admired by scholars and politicians to this day, and he is regarded as one of the greatest thinkers in Indian history.
